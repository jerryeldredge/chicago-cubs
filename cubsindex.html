Chicago Cubs

The Chicago Cubs are a professional baseball team based in Chicago, Illinois. They are a member of the National League (NL) Central division in Major League Baseball (MLB). The Cubs were founded in 1876 and have a long and storied history in the sport.

The Cubs have won three World Series championships, with their most recent victory in 2016, ending a 108-year championship drought. The team plays its home games at Wrigley Field, which is one of the oldest and most iconic ballparks in the United States. The Cubs have a passionate fan base and are known for their loyal supporters, often referred to as "Cubs fans" or "Cubbies." The team's mascot is a bear named "Clark." The Cubs have had many notable players throughout their history, including Ernie Banks, Ryne Sandberg, and Sammy Sosa. The team continues to be a beloved part of Chicago's sports culture and has a rich tradition in Major League Baseball.
